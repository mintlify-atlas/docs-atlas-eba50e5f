## Overview

As your Merchant of Record, Polar must ensure compliance with payment processor requirements, legal regulations, and our commitment to a safe, ethical platform. This policy outlines what products and services can be sold through Polar.

## General Principles

### Allowed

Polar is designed for selling:

- Digital products and software
- Software licenses and subscriptions
- Developer tools and services
- SaaS products
- Digital content (ebooks, courses, templates)
- Access to repositories and code
- Discord access and memberships
- File downloads
- API access
- Professional services related to software

### Focus

Polar is optimized for:

- Open source maintainers
- Independent developers
- Software creators
- Technical creators
- Developer tools companies
- SaaS businesses

## Prohibited Products and Services

The following are **not allowed** on Polar:

### Illegal or Regulated Products

- Illegal goods or services
- Drugs, narcotics, or drug paraphernalia
- Tobacco products
- Alcohol
- Weapons, ammunition, explosives
- Prescription medications
- Unlicensed financial services
- Unlicensed gambling or lottery services
- Pyramid schemes or multi-level marketing

### Harmful or Dangerous Content

- Malware, viruses, or harmful software
- Hacking tools for malicious purposes
- Exploit kits or vulnerability scanners (for malicious use)
- DDoS services or botnets
- Services that facilitate illegal activity
- Content that promotes violence or terrorism
- Content that promotes self-harm

### Intellectual Property Violations

- Pirated or stolen software
- Cracked licenses or key generators
- Copyrighted content without authorization
- Trademark infringements
- Counterfeits or replicas
- Unauthorized reproductions

### Adult Content

- Pornographic material
- Adult entertainment services
- Escort services
- Dating services with adult content

### Deceptive Practices

- Fake reviews or testimonials
- Misleading product claims
- Bait and switch offerings
- Artificially inflated pricing
- Fake scarcity or urgency
- Products that don't deliver as described

### Financial Services

- Cryptocurrency mining services
- Investment advice (unless properly licensed)
- Get-rich-quick schemes
- Binary options trading
- Payday loans
- Debt collection services

### High-Risk Products

- Products with high chargeback rates
- Products that routinely result in disputes
- Services with unpredictable delivery
- Goods that may not arrive as described

## Stripe's Prohibited Businesses

As we use Stripe for payment processing, we must also comply with [Stripe's Prohibited and Restricted Businesses list](https://stripe.com/legal/restricted-businesses). This includes additional categories not listed above.

Violating Stripe's prohibited businesses policy may result in immediate account suspension.

## Product Requirements

### Digital Products

All digital products must:

- **Deliver as Described**: Match marketing materials and descriptions
- **Function Properly**: Work as advertised without critical bugs
- **Be Legal**: Comply with applicable laws and regulations
- **Respect Copyright**: Not infringe on intellectual property
- **Be Safe**: Not contain malware, viruses, or security threats

### Software and Tools

Software products must:

- Include clear documentation
- Specify system requirements
- Provide reasonable support
- Disclose limitations and bugs
- Follow security best practices
- Not be used for malicious purposes

### Subscriptions

Subscription products must:

- Clearly state billing frequency
- Describe what's included
- Offer reasonable cancellation terms
- Deliver consistent value
- Honor refund policies

## Seller Obligations

### Product Accuracy

- **Accurate Descriptions**: Clearly describe what customers receive
- **Honest Marketing**: Don't exaggerate or misrepresent features
- **Clear Pricing**: Display all costs upfront
- **Delivery Timeline**: Set realistic delivery expectations

### Customer Service

- **Respond Promptly**: Reply to customer inquiries within 48 hours
- **Resolve Issues**: Work to resolve disputes fairly
- **Honor Refunds**: Follow your stated refund policy
- **Maintain Quality**: Ensure consistent product quality

### Legal Compliance

- **Follow Laws**: Comply with applicable local and international laws
- **Respect Copyright**: Only sell products you have rights to
- **Privacy Laws**: Handle customer data per GDPR and other regulations
- **Export Controls**: Don't sell to sanctioned countries or entities

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you encounter a product or seller that violates this policy:

1. **Email**: [abuse@polar.sh](mailto:abuse@polar.sh)
2. **Include**: Links, screenshots, and description of violation
3. **Confidential**: Reports are kept confidential
4. **Review**: We review all reports within 48 hours

### What Happens Next

1. **Investigation**: We review the reported content
2. **Action**: If violation confirmed, we take appropriate action
3. **Notification**: Reporter notified of outcome (if appropriate)

## Enforcement

### Review Process

Polar may review accounts and products:

- During [automated reviews](/merchant-of-record/account-reviews)
- In response to reports
- During routine audits
- Based on transaction patterns
- Following customer complaints

### Warning

For first-time or minor violations:

1. Email notification sent
2. Specific violation explained
3. Correction timeline provided
4. Account monitored for compliance

### Suspension

For serious or repeated violations:

1. Account temporarily suspended
2. Payouts held pending investigation
3. Products removed from platform
4. Opportunity to appeal provided

### Termination

For severe or unresolved violations:

1. Account permanently terminated
2. Access to platform revoked
3. Final payout processed (minus any disputes)
4. No reinstatement possible

## Appeals Process

### If Your Account is Suspended

1. **Review Email**: Read the suspension notification carefully
2. **Gather Evidence**: Compile documentation proving compliance
3. **Submit Appeal**: Email [support@polar.sh](mailto:support@polar.sh) with:
   - Account details
   - Explanation of circumstances
   - Evidence of compliance
   - Corrective actions taken
4. **Wait for Review**: We respond within 5 business days

### Appeal Outcomes

- **Approved**: Account reinstated, guidance provided
- **Approved with Conditions**: Specific requirements to meet
- **Denied**: Suspension upheld, explanation provided

## Special Cases

### Open Source Projects

Open source maintainers can sell:

- Premium support
- Commercial licenses
- Priority bug fixes
- Feature requests
- Documentation
- Training

As long as these don't violate core principles.

### Educational Content

Educational products are welcome:

- Programming courses
- Technical tutorials
- Documentation
- Training materials
- Workshops and webinars

Must deliver educational value and not be misleading.

### Beta or Early Access

Beta products are acceptable if:

- Clearly labeled as beta
- Bugs and limitations disclosed
- Price reflects early state
- Regular updates provided
- Support is available

### Professional Services

Software-related services allowed:

- Consulting
- Code reviews
- Pair programming
- Architecture design
- Technical advisory

Must be delivered as agreed and within reasonable timeframe.

## Policy Updates

### Changes to This Policy

Polar may update this policy:

- To comply with new regulations
- Based on platform evolution
- In response to emerging issues
- Following partner requirements (e.g., Stripe)

### Notification

When policy changes:

1. **Email Notice**: Sent to all active sellers
2. **Effective Date**: Minimum 30 days notice
3. **Continued Use**: Constitutes acceptance
4. **Right to Discontinue**: You may close account before changes take effect

## Questions About Acceptable Use

### Before You Start Selling

Unsure if your product is allowed?

1. Review this policy carefully
2. Check [Stripe's restricted businesses](https://stripe.com/legal/restricted-businesses)
3. Email [support@polar.sh](mailto:support@polar.sh) with:
   - Product description
   - Target market
   - How it works
   - Any concerns

### Gray Areas

Some products may fall in gray areas:

- Security research tools
- Penetration testing services
- Controversial but legal content
- High-risk product categories

Contact us before listing to avoid issues.
